# Code of Conduct

SicarioSpec is a security and governance project. Discussion should be direct,
technical, and respectful.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat contributors, users, and maintainers with respect.
- Assume good intent while still requiring evidence for technical claims.
- Keep criticism focused on code, documentation, controls, and project outcomes.
- Report security-sensitive issues privately through the security policy.
- Avoid sharing secrets, private tenant data, customer data, or proprietary
  control text in public issues, pull requests, discussions, or logs.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Publicly disclosing vulnerabilities before maintainers have had a reasonable
  chance to respond.
- Repeatedly derailing technical threads after maintainer redirection.
- Misrepresenting SicarioSpec as a certification, legal opinion, audit opinion,
  or guarantee of secure code.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, issues, discussions, and
pull requests that violate this code. Maintainers may also temporarily or
permanently restrict participation for repeated or severe violations.

When possible, maintainers should explain the decision and provide a path back
to productive participation. Severe abuse, doxxing, threats, or active
exploitation attempts may be escalated immediately to the hosting platform.

## Reporting

For conduct issues, contact the maintainers through GitHub. For vulnerabilities
or sensitive security concerns, follow `SECURITY.md` instead of opening a public
issue.
